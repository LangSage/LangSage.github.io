LexiLand Russian

Clickable topic plan for a free mobile-first survival vocabulary game. No grammar course. Words are learned through sound, picture, action and real situations.

Open topicsMVP levels

Target

Adults who live in a Russian-speaking city and need useful words for daily life.

Method

Audio + picture + action + real situation. Grammar stays hidden inside ready chunks.

Goal

First 100–150 useful words/chunks, enough to start understanding language around them.

🧭 1. First survival words MVP

Purpose: unlock basic meaning before any topic course.

yes/nohere/therewhat/wherecan/cannotneedhelpstop/gowater/food
I do not understand.
Help, please.
Repeat, please.
  • Introduce with sound, icon and one action.
  • Game: hear and tap the object.
  • Game: stop/go danger path.
  • Game: yes/no choice scene.
➡️ 2. Movement and directions MVP

Purpose: make Russian control the character.

gostopherethererightleftstraightbackfastslow
  • Introduce one command at a time.
  • Game: maze controlled by audio commands.
  • Game: wrong-turn trap.
  • Game: guide another character.
🆘 3. Help and repair phrases MVP

Purpose: give the learner power when speech is too fast or unclear.

Help, please.
I do not understand.
Slowly, please.
Show me, please.
  • Teach as whole phrases.
  • Game: NPC speaks fast; player chooses slow/repeat.
  • Game: lost object; player asks to show.
🛒 4. Shop and food MVP

Purpose: buy basic food and water.

breadwatermilkricemeatteabag

How much is it?
Do you need a bag?
  • Introduce 6 basic items first.
  • Game: basket mission.
  • Game: cashier yes/no scene.
  • Game: find three items on shelves.

North star

LexiLand Russian should be a bridge from zero to first understanding. A learner should play for five minutes and leave with one useful phrase they can use today.
